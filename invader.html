A-26C Invader Tail # 44-35523 (N3428G)

Significant Facts

1. Air Force used this aircraft as a trainer (1945 - 1946) and reconnaissance plane (1956 - 1958)

2. Flew as an Aerial Tanker with several civilian companies from the 1960's to 1985

3. Transferred from Bonanzaville Air Museum in East Fargo, North Dakota. When the NMUSAF instituted a program requiring civilian museums to pay for liability insurance on display aircraft, they turned it back into the system.

4. Disassembled and moved to Omaha by Worldwide Aircraft Flew to Dover on a C-17 training mission.

5. Painted in the generic markings of an A-26 assigned to Dover AAF at the 4146th Rocket Test Center.

History

Date Event
10 May 1945 Manufactured by Douglas Aircraft, Tulsa, Oklahoma and delivered to the US Army Air Force
May 1945 To 140th Army Air Force Base Unit (Air Training Command) Moody Army Air Force, Georgia
October 1945 To 138th Army Air Force Base Unit (Continental Air Force) Lake Charles Army Air Field, Louisiana (assigned to 47th Bombardment [Medium] Group)
March 1946 To 307th Army Air Force Base Unit (Tactical Air Command) Lake Charles Army Air Field, Louisiana
May 1946 To 4160th Air Force Base Unit (Air Material Command), Hill Air Force Base, Utah (storage to RB-2gC)
April 1952 To 363rd Tactical Reconnaissance Wing (TAC), Shaw Air Force Base, South Carolina
April 1954 To 432nd Tactical Reconnaissance Group (TAC), Shaw Air Force Base, South Carolina
May 1956 To 183rd Tactical Reconnaissance (Night Photograph) Squadron (Air National Guard), Hawkins Field, Jackson, Mississippi
March 1958 To Arizona Aircraft Storage Branch (Air Mobility Command), Davis-Monthan Air Force Base, Arizona
August 2008 Arrived at the Air Mobility Command Museum for restoration
