Nevada Air National Guard

1997 - 1999

Reno, Nevada

Aerial Port

In September 1997, I joined the Air National Guard located at the Reno International Airport. The Aerial Port handles all manner of duties associated with air travel. Most of the positions you see at the airport are handled by Aerial Port i.e. Passenger Service, Food Service/Crew & Passenger Meals, and Cargo Loading. In Reno, the main mission was Air Drop which centered around dropping pallets or personnel. We used sandbags that simulated personnel and pallets of different weights that simulated different configurations of cargo to be dropped. When the mission is dropping pallets, someone has to be at the drop location to pick up the pallets, load them on a truck and drive them back to the base. I operated both equipment (forklifts) to pick up the pallets and load them on the 40' trailer, and the tractor trailer to haul the pallets back to the base. In my position, I operated loaders (flat topped vehicles with a driver cab) used to load pallets onto aircraft, fork lifts of various sizes, tractor trailer with 40' flat bed used for return transportation of pallets after they were dropped, and other (civilian-type) vehicles.
